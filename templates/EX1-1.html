Adidas (stylized as ɑdidɑs since 1949) is a footwear, sportswear and sports equipment company founded
in Germany. It is named after its founder, Adolf Dassler, also known by his nickname Adi, who began producing sneakers in the 1920s, together with his brother Rudolf Dassler, in Herzogenaurach, near
Nuremberg. The name "adidas" is a combination of Adolf's pseudonym, Adi, and the beginning of his surname, Dassler.
The company created by the brothers was founded in 1920, however, it was divided
in 1948, giving rise to Adidas and also to Puma, a rival company founded by Rudolf Dassler, Adi's brother.
